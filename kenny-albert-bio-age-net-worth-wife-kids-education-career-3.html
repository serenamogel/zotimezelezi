Kenny Albert Bio, Age, Net Worth, Wife/Kids, Education, Career

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, July 18, 2024

Kenny Albert Biography

Kenny Albert is an American Sportscaster. He is currently the only sportscaster to cover all four major professional sports leagues in the U.S. and Canada: the NBA, NFL, NHL, and MLB. He also gives a play by play analysis of the game. Albert is the son of sportscaster Marv Albert. His uncles are Al Albert and Steve Albert who are both sportscasters too.

At 16, he wrote content for the Rangers program. Growing up apart from his father, his idol was Vancouver Canucks play-by-play broadcaster, Jim Robson. From 1981 to 1986, Albert, growing up in Sands Point, covered high school sports for the Port Washington News at Paul D. Schreiber High School, an Anton Community Newspapers publication.

Kenny Albert Age

Albert is aged 52 years as of 2020. He was born on February 2, 1968, in New York City, United States. He celebrates his birthday on the 2nd of February every year.

Kenny Albert Education

Albert went to New York University where he graduated with a Bachelor’s Degree in broadcasting and journalism. He was a member of the Sigma Phi Epsilon fraternity which is commonly known as SigEp, it’s a social college fraternity for male college students.

Kenny Albert Family

Albert was born in New York City to his parents, Marv Albert and Benita Oberlander. His father Marv Albert is a sportscaster who was commonly known as “the voice of Basketball” from 1967 to 2004 and was also referred to as “the Voice of the New York Knicks”. He currently serves as a lead announcer for NBA games for Turner Sports. His uncles are sportscasters Al Albert and Steve Albert.

On his fifth birthday, his parents gave him a tape recorder so he could practice his broadcasting and on his sixth birthday, his father took him along to a New York Rangers game. During the game, one of the statisticians had to leave and Albert got the chance to do the stats for the rest of the game. At the age of 14, Albert became the official statistician for the Rangers on the radio.

Kenny Albert Wife

Kenny Albert has been happily married to his wife Barbara Wolf since 1996. Albert was introduced to his wife by a close friend and Baltimore sports reporter, Jerry Coleman. He currently lives in New Jersey with his wife of 20 years and their two daughters, Amanda and Sydney.

Kenny Albert Net Worth

Albert the American sportscaster has a net worth of $2 million. His main source of income is from his career as a sportscaster.

Kenny Albert Broadcast Career

Albert is the radio voice of the New York Rangers, as well as a play-by-play announcer and field-level reporter for Fox’s coverage of Major League Baseball, the NFL and previously, the Sugar Bowl. Previously, he handled TV play-by-play for the Washington Capitals and Washington Bullets (now the Washington Wizards) and was a part-time announcer on Washington Nationals telecasts in 2005.

Additionally, he does TV play-by-play for Washington Redskins preseason games with Joe Theismann. Albert called the international broadcast of Super Bowl XLVI with Theismann. When Fox had the network contract for the National Hockey League in the 1990s, Albert also worked on Fox NHL Saturday telecasts. Albert now does play-by-play for the NHL on NBC and formerly with Versus (now called NBCSN).

Albert called Game 1 of the 2014 Stanley Cup Finals for NBC, filling in for Doc Emrick, who was dealing with a death in the family. He has done work for NBC’s Olympics coverage, as a play-by-play announcer for men’s and women’s ice hockey at every Winter Olympic Games since Salt Lake City in 2002.

Albert has also done college basketball for ESPN Plus and is a substitute play-by-play announcer for televised New York Knicks games on MSG Network. For the 2011 playoffs, Albert broadcast for two playoff teams in the same market, doing the play-by-play for the New York Rangers on WEPN 1050 ESPN radio and filling in on MSG Network doing play-by-play for the New York Knicks.

Albert is known to some Chicago sports fans as “The Kiss of Death” to their teams. Many games involving the Bears and Blackhawks with Albert announcing have ended in losses for both teams. Examples include Game 7 of the 2014 Western Conference Final between the Blackhawks and Kings and many Chicago Bears’ games with Albert announcing since 2004.

Albert was the lead play-by-play announcer for the 2015 American League Division Series between the Texas Rangers and the Toronto Blue Jays. At the top of the 7th inning of Game 5, he helped explain the rule regarding the errant throw by Toronto Blue Jays catcher Russell Martin, which resulted in Texas scoring the go-ahead run. At the bottom of the inning, he called Jose Bautista’s go-ahead home run.

In 2016, he was nominated for the Sports Emmy Award for Outstanding Sports Personality, Play-by-Play. The list included his own father, fellow NFL on Fox announcer Kevin Burkhardt, NHL announcer and winner Mike Emrick.

Albert on October 25, 2009, called the play-by-play of the Minnesota Vikings – Pittsburgh Steelers NFL game for Fox and then hosted the New York Yankees’ locker room celebration after clinching the American League Championship Series that night. The following night he broadcast a Rangers game on the radio and on October 28, he called the play-by-play of the New York Knicks season opener on MSG.
