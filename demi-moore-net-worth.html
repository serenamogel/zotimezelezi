Charity

Thorn: Digital Defenders of Children is a non-profit organization founded by Demi Moore and Ashton Kutcher. It works to address and prevent sexual exploitation and human trafficking. The foundation was earlier known as the “DNA Foundation.”

In 2010, Moore won $250,000 from the challenge held by Pepsi for the celebrities. She donated the winning money to GEMS, an organization that mentors and empowers young women who have been subjected to any kind of sexual harm and trafficking.

The same year, she traveled to Haiti to help the survivors of the 2010 earthquake. She was joined by other fellow celebrities too.

Demi supports a non-profit organization named Chrysalis, which provides employment opportunities to the destitute.

In 2013, the actress teamed with CNN Freedom Project for a documentary that uncovered Nepal’s sex trade. It was an initiative by the 2010’s CNN Hero Anuradha Koirala. The documentary was named “Nepal’s Stolen Children.”

Movies and TV shows

Rose to fame from her groundbreaking role in the 1990 film Ghost, Demi Moore started to prove her prominence in the Hollywood Industry. That year, it became the highest-grossing film, and Moore received a Golden Globe nomination.

Career

Born as Demetria Gene Guynes on 11th November 1962, Demi never had a normal family and faced many problems from the beginning.

Her parents were alcoholics and drenched in debts, therefore constantly moving to new places.

Just after two months of marriage, her biological father, Charles Harmon Senior, left her pregnant mother, after which Demi was raised by an abusive stepfather, Dan Guynes.

Furthermore, her own mother was never supportive of her who even once let a man rape Demi for $500.

Demi had a seriously rough childhood. At the age of 16, she dropped out of high school and moved to become a model.

She debuted in the acting field with a small role in the 1981 film- Choices.

Her first breakthrough was the 1990 movie Ghost, after which she starred in numerous hits, and her career escalated.

Personal Life

Demi got her surname from her first husband, Freddy Moore; she married in 1980 and, after five years, divorced. Then, the actress was engaged to Emilio Estevez, but they later called off their wedding.

Likewise, in 1987, Demi Moore married Bruce Willis, and this power couple was blessed with three daughters; Rumer (born 1988), Scout (born 1991), and Tallulah (born 1994). After eleven years of marriage, the couple announced their divorce and got separated in October 2020.

Before her third marriage, the actress dated Oliver Whitecomb, a martial artist, for three years.

Her third marriage was with That 70s Show‘s actor Ashton Kutcher. The pair started dating in 2003 and got married in 2005. The couple announced their separation in November 2011 and eventually got divorced in 2013.
