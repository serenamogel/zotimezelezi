Biography

William Alexander was born in Germany on April 2, 1915. German painter and art instructor best known for launching and hosting the PBS television programs The Art of Bill Alexander and The Magic of Oil Painting. He instructed Bob Ross in oil painting and inspired Ross’ own television art program, The Joy of Painting. After being drafted into the German army during the Second World War, Alexander immigrated to the United States with the help of his Allied captors, many of whose wives sat for portraits with him.

Career

Alexander was recruited into the Wehrmacht during World War II as a carriage builder apprentice. He was captured by Allied troops and painted pictures of Allied soldiers’ wives before making his way to America. Alexander was a refugee after WWII and went on to become a skilled painter. He traveled to North America after inventing the modern “quick” version of the 15th century wet-on-wet technique.
