Neil Patrick Harris Career:

Patrick Harris rose to prominence as a notable American actor. Mark Medoff, an actor and playwright, discovered him when he was 15 and cast him in a movie version of the novel Clara’s Heart. He received a “Golden Globe” nomination for his role in the film.

After a successful beginning to his career, he acted in the 1988 children’s fantasy novel Purple People Eater. The lead role in ‘Doogie Howser, M.D.’ on ABC boosted his career. The show’s success solidified his position as a leader in his field. Television audiences realized him.

In the 1995 thriller “Animal Room,” he made his adult film debut as a teenager. The Next Best Thing, Undercover Brother, and Starship Troopers were just a few of the films he appeared in after that. In 2003, he joined the cast of the hit musical “Cabaret,” performing alongside stars like Deborah Gibson and Tom Bosley.

The acclaim he received directly resulted from his stellar performance. In 2005, he played womanizer Barney Stinson on the CBS sitcom “How I Met Your Mother.” He kept up his portrayal of the part until 2014. In 2008, he was nominated for a “Primetime Emmy” as “Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series.”

He directed a production of the rock musical “Rent” at the “Hollywood Bowl” in 2010. The following year, he played the lead role in “Company,” a musical by Stephen Sondheim. He also oversaw the production of “The Expert at the Card Table” at California’s Broad Stage’s Edye in 2011.

That same year, he voiced the character of Patrick Winslow in the CGI comedy film The Smurfs. He then appeared in the sequel, The Smurf 2, once again playing the role of Patrick Winslow. In the 2013 sequel to “Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs, he provided the voice of’ Steve the Monkey.’

Movies like “A Million Ways to Die in the West” and “Gone Girl” featured him the following year. He hosted, and executive produced the live television variety show “Best Time Ever with Neil Patrick Harris” in 2015. The T.V. show based on ‘A Series of Unfortunate Events’ starred Neil and aired from 2017 to 2019.
