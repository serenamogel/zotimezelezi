Tyrese Maxey- Biography

Tyrese Maxey is an American professional basketball player with the Philadelphia 76ers of the National Basketball Association. He is a shooting guard. He had a tremendous college basketball career and was selected 21st overall in the 2020 NBA draft.

Tyrese Maxey – Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Parents

Tyrese was born on November 4, 2000, to a Christian family in Dallas, Texas, and is an African-American. He has always been athletic and has played basketball since he was a child. Tyrese hasn’t revealed much about his parents and family, however, his father’s name is Tyrone, and he was the Director of Recruiting for the 2017 SMU Mustang Men’s Basketball season, while his mother’s name is Denise Maxey. Denasia, Talia, and Keira are his three sisters, and he is currently 20 years old.

Tyrese hasn’t revealed much about his early education, however, he did mention that he played basketball in middle school. He received his high school diploma from South Garland High School, where he also played basketball. Tyrese later enrolled at the University of Kentucky in 2019 to further his education and play college basketball. Tyrese Maxey- Professional Career

Tyrese had a successful high school basketball career, playing for the University of Kentucky’s team. He set a record of 22.5 average points and 3.1 assists in his junior year, leading his club to a 36-4 record. Then, in his senior year, he averaged 22.2 points per game with 3.6 assists, leading the squad to yet another record. Throughout his high school career, he was a catalyst for the team, and at the end of the season, Rivals, 246 Sports, and ESPN all gave him a 5-star recruit rating.

Tyrese went on to join the Kentucky Wildcats, where he scored 26 points in his debut season, leading the team to a triumph over Michigan State, the number one team in the country. He was chosen to the SEC Freshman All-Star team. He did not return to college the following year, opting instead to enter the 2020 NBA Draft. In 2020, he was named to the SEC Second All-Team. Tyrese is well-known for winning a gold medal with the United States U-18 squad in the 2018 FIBA U-18 American Championship.

In the 21st overall selection, he was selected by the Philadelphia 76ers, and in 2020-2021, he began his debut season as a professional player at shooting guard. Tyrese had the greatest point total by an NBA rookie since 1970, with 39 points against the Denver Nuggets. He possessed tremendous offensive and shooting talents, but he didn’t get much playing time in the NBA owing to his lack of experience. He practiced and trained on his weak points while also learning new talents, and his minutes finally increased in tandem with his average.
